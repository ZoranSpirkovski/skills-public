# think-acronym

Van Clief's early workflow acronym for academic AI use, introduced in the January 2024 Flagler workshop. T: Thoughts to Thematics, using voice transcription to dump ideas and have an AI organize them. H: Heuristic Helper, using AI to create connections beyond the initial thematic pass. I: Integrate Data, asking AI to extend a draft with data, studies, or a different field's frame. N: Navigational Nemesis, telling AI to disagree, argue against your position, and break the echo chamber. K: Knowledge Amplification, asking for other perspectives, bias checks, and better context. The 2026 companion frames THINK as Layer 0 thinking that later grew into the folder architecture and the Model Workspace Protocol.

## Claims

- THINK is a five-step workflow: Thoughts to Thematics, Heuristic Helper, Integrate Data, Navigational Nemesis, Knowledge Amplification [sources/archive-2-1-mindset-before-method]
- THINK describes a conversation with a chatbot and fits beginner academic writing work [sources/archive-2-1-mindset-before-method]
- The companion reframes THINK as "Layer 0 thinking" relative to the folder architecture and five-layer context hierarchy [sources/archive-2-1-mindset-before-method]
- The Navigational Nemesis step (ask the AI to argue against you) is the earliest version of what Van Clief now teaches as adversarial context design [sources/archive-2-1-mindset-before-method]
- THINK is also an early instance of computational orchestration without the name: it routes conversational work through discrete AI tasks [sources/archive-2-1-mindset-before-method]
