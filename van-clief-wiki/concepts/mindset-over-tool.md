# mindset-over-tool

Van Clief's central argument across the Archive Module 2 videos. AI in education and business is not a tool problem. It is a mindset problem. The fundamentals of the work have not changed. What changed is that the baseline output is now cheap and shared. The expert's interpretation, perspective, and judgment become the scarce asset. The practical consequence: stop asking "how do we stop students from using it" and start asking "what did our assignments actually measure if an AI can clear the bar." Holds across education, professional services, and research.

## Claims

- AI is a mindset problem, not a tool problem [sources/archive-2-1-mindset-before-method]
- The fundamentals of education have not changed; the baseline of what is expected has risen [sources/archive-2-1-mindset-before-method] [sources/archive-2-2-raise-the-bar]
- When everyone shares the same base output, the expert's unique perspective becomes the scarce resource [sources/archive-2-1-mindset-before-method] [sources/archive-2-3-more-human]
- "You don't need to use AI to have AI in the classroom." The thinking is required; the tool is optional [sources/archive-2-1-mindset-before-method]
- Tasks AI handles well were already formulaic before AI arrived. The tasks still requiring humans are the tasks that mattered [sources/archive-2-3-more-human]
- Across 600+ professionals trained (Pacific Life, Colgate-Palmolive, KPMG UK), the people who thrive with AI are the ones who already know their domain [sources/archive-2-1-mindset-before-method] [sources/archive-2-3-more-human]
