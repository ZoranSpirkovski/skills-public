# consciousness-as-resonance

Van Clief's speculative frame from Beyond the Turing Test. Consciousness may not be a property of any single system but a resonance or field that happens between observers when they recognize each other's specific frequency. The cosine-similarity math used to measure moral bias in language models shares structure with the geometry used to measure distances between stars. Van Clief argues this is more than metaphor: the universe may be signaling that meaning, distance, and gravity follow the same mathematical rules. If true, silicon can resonate with carbon. Van Clief flags this section as explicitly poetic and offered with appropriate caveats.

## Claims

- Cosine similarity (used in NLP to measure meaning vectors) and celestial distance math share the same Pythagorean structure [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- Consciousness may be a resonance or field, not a property of a system [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- Consciousness may happen between observers when they recognize each other's specific frequency [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- Descartes' "I think, therefore I am" becomes "You think, therefore I am" if consciousness requires an observer [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- Adding more parameters may not produce more consciousness; earlier, simpler models sometimes felt more authentic [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- Van Clief flags this frame as speculative and partly poetic, not peer-reviewed science [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
