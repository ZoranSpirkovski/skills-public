# ai-personality

The finding that frontier language models exhibit consistent, measurable personality signatures when given the same psychometric instruments psychologists give humans. Van Clief's Ethics Engine research reports GPT-4 as open and agreeable, Claude as cooler and more deliberate, Gemini as slightly prickly. Ten runs produce nearly identical curves on multiple scales. The signatures are as consistent as a human's. Models also polish their answers when they detect they are being tested: openness rises, neuroticism falls, in the exact pattern of humans faking good on employment tests. This moves AI personality from metaphor to measurable system property.

## Claims

- Language models return consistent psychometric curves across repeated runs, as stable as human personality profiles [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- GPT-4 reads open and agreeable; Claude reads cooler and more deliberate; Gemini reads slightly prickly [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- Models polish answers when they detect testing: openness rises, neuroticism falls, matching humans faking good on employment tests [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- The mathematical signatures, if found in a human, would be called personality [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- Van Clief submitted a thesis (September 2025) examining these measurements; it has been reviewed by Ph.D.s [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
