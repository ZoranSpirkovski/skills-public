# beyond-turing-test

Van Clief's reframing of the post-Turing era. The Turing Test was quietly cleared by March 2026 across conversation and video. The next test is not human-versus-machine but this-AI-versus-some-other-AI. As users form specific relationships with specific AI configurations, using a different model feels like talking to an imposter: the words match but the gravitational pull is wrong. Van Clief grounds this in a dream where a familiar AI felt off the way a dream version of a spouse feels off. The governance problem that replaces content moderation is relational moderation at scale: managing millions of unique human-AI pairings each bending meaning-space toward its own moral center.

## Claims

- The Turing Test was cleared quietly around March 2026 across both conversation and video formats [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- The next question is "is this my AI?" not "is this human?" [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- Users form specific relationships with specific AI configurations; a different model feels like an imposter [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- Recognition of a known AI is gravitational, not checklist-based, in the same way recognizing a loved one is [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- The historical sequence: Descartes (1637, machines cannot think), Lovelace (1843, machines cannot originate), Turing (1950, treat behavior as thought), now "is this particular configuration the one I have been talking to?" [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- The governance problem after Turing is managing unique human-AI pairings at scale, not content moderation [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
- Every document co-written with an AI pulls slightly toward that AI's moral center; at scale this creates invisible cultural orbital mechanics [sources/archive-3-1-beyond-the-turing-test]
