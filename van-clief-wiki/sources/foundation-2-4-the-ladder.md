# foundation-2-4-the-ladder

> Packaged inside `.wiki/raw/the-foundation.md`, lines ~736-849. Foundation Module 2, Lesson 4.

## Summary

Value always lives above whatever just got commoditized. A Fortune 500 company spent $200K building a custom AI document workflow; the platform added the same feature natively and the investment evaporated. Van Clief introduces a three-level abstraction ladder for AI work using books, movies, and video games as analogues: Layer 1 (prompts in, outputs out) is the book layer (commoditized); Layer 2 (flows connecting prompts to processes) is the movie layer; Layer 3 (systems that learn from the data their own flows generate) is the video-game layer. A web agency case shows the migration: give away the book layer, charge for the bespoke layer above.

## Key claims

1. A Fortune 500 company spent $200,000 on a custom AI document workflow that a platform update made free one release later.
2. The three-level ladder of AI use: Layer 1 typing prompts (book), Layer 2 building flows (movie), Layer 3 systems that learn from their own data (video game).
3. "Don't automate what the next model will give you for free" - invest in what is specific to your context, your data, your relationships.
4. A web agency cut its basic HTML work to near-zero using Lovable-class generators, kept charging $20K/month for design strategy, research, and judgment that cannot be scraped.
5. Automation kills specific middleman positions; the underlying markets are as durable as human desire.
6. AI may grow markets more than it shrinks them if it collapses the broken discovery layer (SEO, ad spend, attention middlemen) between producer and consumer.
7. The folder architecture from [[foundation-1-2-your-first-folder]] is an example of building at Layer 2: context structuring specific to the user's work that no general tool can do.
