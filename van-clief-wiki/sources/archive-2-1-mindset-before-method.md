# archive-2-1-mindset-before-method

## Summary

Lesson 2.1 pairs a 39-minute workshop Van Clief gave to Flagler College professors in January 2024 with a 2026 written companion. The core thesis: AI in education is a mindset problem, not a tool problem. When everyone shares the same base output, the expert's unique perspective becomes the scarce asset. Van Clief walks through a multidisciplinary history of thinking machines (Aristotle, Al-Jazari, Roger Bacon, Descartes, Lovelace, Turing) to argue that AI belongs in every classroom, not just computer science. He introduces the THINK acronym (Thoughts to Thematics, Heuristic Helper, Integrate Data, Navigational Nemesis, Knowledge Amplification) as a starting process. The companion marks THINK as Layer 0 thinking that later grew into the folder architecture and the Model Workspace Protocol.

## Key claims

1. AI is a mindset problem, not a tool problem. Fundamentals of education have not changed; the baseline just rose.
2. Three structural problems face educators: AI overwhelm, tool deficiency, and community void. Each became a product line: training, EdubaWare, and Clief Notes.
3. "You don't need to use AI to have AI in the classroom." The thinking is required; the tool is optional.
4. The calculator analogy applies: teachers learned when to allow and when to restrict; they did not ban.
5. The PhD dissertation thought experiment reframes the problem. If a board unknowingly approves an AI-written dissertation, the question becomes whether the system's bar was low enough for a machine to clear, not whether AI should exist.
6. The history of thinking machines (Aristotle's Politics, Al-Jazari, Roger Bacon's Opus Majus, Descartes, Lovelace, Turing) supports a multidisciplinary case for AI in every field.
7. The FunSearch paper (Fawzi, Romera) is mathematical proof that language models can produce novel solutions, cited as a decade-old unsolved function the system cracked after training on the solution method.
8. Prompt engineering is the least important layer of effective AI work. Architecture, context structure, and knowing which layer a problem belongs on matter more.
9. The THINK acronym was a starting point and is now framed as Layer 0 thinking relative to the folder architecture, the five-layer context hierarchy, and the Model Workspace Protocol.
10. The F-35 anecdote (pilot ejects, plane keeps flying and lands) is closer to advanced autopilot than modern LLMs; the distinction matters more in 2026 than in January 2024.
