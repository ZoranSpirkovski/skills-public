# stack-2-3-mobile-workflow-patterns

> Van Clief classroom, course "Building Your Stack", Module 2 Lesson 3. Raw file at `.wiki/raw/stack/lesson-2-3-mobile-workflow-patterns.md`. Video placeholder; lesson companion is canonical.

## Summary

Working from your phone is a different mode, not a smaller version of desk work. The goal is to do the right things from mobile, not everything. Three mobile modes: check and acknowledge (10-second status glance), quick intervention (one or two short messages at a decision point), voice-driven work (longer instructions spoken instead of typed). Mobile works for monitoring, binary decisions, short responses, voice input, approvals, reading outputs. Mobile does not work for deep editing, multi-file context, long-form writing, debugging, or nuanced creative direction. The handoff pattern: tell Claude you are stepping away and to flag blockers clearly; when you return, ask Claude to summarize progress and needed decisions.

## Key claims

1. Mobile is a different mode of work. Slower keyboard, less on-screen context, split attention. But voice input, quick access, and presence in moments that would otherwise be idle.
2. Three mobile modes. Mode 1: check and acknowledge (status glance, 10 seconds). Mode 2: quick intervention (one or two short messages). Mode 3: voice-driven work (longer instructions spoken).
3. Mode 2 examples: "proceed with that approach", "use blue not green", "skip that section for now", "stop, I'll fix when I'm back".
4. Voice input beats mobile typing. The Claude app accepts voice. The phone keyboard also accepts voice.
5. What works on mobile: monitoring, binary decisions, short responses, voice instructions, approving actions, reading outputs.
6. What does not work on mobile: deep editing, multi-file context, long-form writing, debugging, nuanced creative direction.
7. The handoff pattern from desk to mobile: "I'm stepping away. Continue with the build. If you hit a blocker, describe it and wait. I'll check in from my phone."
8. The handoff pattern from mobile to desk: "What happened while I was away? Summarize progress and any decisions you need from me."
9. Voice input tips: speak in complete thoughts, use natural language, pause for punctuation, review before sending, keep it short.
10. The best mobile workflow is often not checking at all. Mobile access is a capability, not an obligation.
11. Check when the task has decision points Claude cannot make alone, when something is about to finish, or when you gave ambiguous input. Wait otherwise.
