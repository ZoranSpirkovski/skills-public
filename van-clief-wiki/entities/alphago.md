# alphago

Google DeepMind's Go-playing AI system. Defeated [[lee-sedol]] 4-1 in Seoul in March 2016 and later defeated world #1 [[ke-jie]] 3-0. Trained on 160,000 human games before improving via self-play. Notable for Move 37 in Game 2 vs. Sedol - a move AlphaGo calculated had a 1-in-10,000 probability of being played by a human, which commentators first thought was an error and then recognized as genius.

## Claims

- Beat Lee Sedol 4-1, March 2016, Seoul Four Seasons Hotel [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Move 37 (Game 2): probability 1-in-10,000 for a human to play; Sedol left the room for 15 minutes after seeing it [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Learned from 160,000 human games before moving to self-play [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Defeated Ke Jie 3-0 in 2017; Ke Jie: "not a single human has touched the edge of the truth of Go" [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
