# lee-sedol

South Korean Go grandmaster, one of the strongest players of his generation. Lost 4-1 to [[alphago]] in Seoul, March 2016. His Game 4 win (Move 78, the "God's Move") was humanity's only victory in the match. Retired from professional Go in November 2019, citing AI as "an entity that cannot be defeated."

## Claims

- Predicted a landslide win before the match; lost 4-1 [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Move 78 in Game 4 - the "God's Move" - dropped AlphaGo's evaluation from 70% to below 50%; the one human win [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- After Move 37: "I thought AlphaGo was based on probability calculation and that it was merely a machine. But when I saw this move, I changed my mind. Surely, AlphaGo is creative." [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Retired November 2019: AI is "an entity that cannot be defeated" [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
