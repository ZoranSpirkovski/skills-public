# garry-kasparov

Former World Chess Champion, lost to IBM's Deep Blue in 1997. Cited in Archive 1.2 for his reaction to [[alphazero]]'s chess style: "It's like discovering the secret notebooks of some great player from the past." Van Clief uses him as a bridge between the pre-AI chess world (Deep Blue as brute-force calculator) and AlphaZero (self-taught, dynamic, intuitive).

## Claims

- Lost to Deep Blue in 1997 [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- On AlphaZero: "I can't disguise my satisfaction that it plays with a very dynamic style, much like my own! It's like discovering the secret notebooks of some great player from the past." [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
