# jacques-de-vaucanson

French engineer, 18th century. Built a flute-playing automaton in 1737 that actually played twelve songs through real breath (leather bellows), real fingers (leather-covered), and a moving tongue. Also built the infamous Digesting Duck, which ate, drank, and excreted - later revealed as a fraud by magician Jean-Eugène Robert-Houdin in 1844.

## Claims

- 1737 flute player: twelve songs, real breath and fingers, "musicians complained it played shrilly, but it played" [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Digesting Duck: pre-stored green-dyed breadcrumb "feces" released while eaten grain collected separately - fraud, but advanced public discussion of automata [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Voltaire on Vaucanson's duck: "Without the voice of le Maure and Vaucanson's duck, you would have nothing to remind you of the glory of France" [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Duck burned in a Krakow museum fire ~1879, leaving only heat-warped wings [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]

## Relationships

- Precedes [[pierre-jaquet-droz]] in the 18th-century European automaton tradition
