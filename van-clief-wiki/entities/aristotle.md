# aristotle

Greek philosopher, 4th century BCE. Van Clief cites him in the Flagler workshop as the earliest traceable thinker on autonomous tools. In Book 1 Part 4 of *The Politics*, Aristotle considers what happens when an artificial instrument (a pitchfork, a torch) performs its task without a human operator. Van Clief notes Aristotle used the thought experiment partly to justify slavery, but the structural question (what ownership or moral status attaches to an object that acts on its own) has echoed forward 2,400 years.

- In *The Politics* Book 1 Part 4, Aristotle considers autonomous tools performing human work without operators [sources/archive-2-1-mindset-before-method]
