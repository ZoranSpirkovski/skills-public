# charles-babbage

English mathematician and inventor, 19th century. Designer of the never-built Analytical Engine (1834+), a general-purpose mechanical computer with Mill (processor), Store (memory), and input/output devices - the same architecture still used today, just in brass and steel. Adopted [[joseph-marie-jacquard]]'s punched cards and split them into three types: operation, variable, and number cards.

## Claims

- Conceived the Analytical Engine in 1834 after the Difference Engine stalled [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Architecture (Mill / Store / input / output) is the same pattern a modern desktop uses - "just made of brass and steel instead of silicon" [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Adopted Jacquard's punched cards, separating operation, variable, and number cards - foundational for stored-program computing [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Refined designs for 40 years, six phases of evolution, ~120,000 words of notes - but the engine was never built during his lifetime [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Science Museum London built his Difference Engine No. 2 between 1985 and 1991 using only Babbage-era techniques; it calculates perfectly [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Cited in Van Clief's "one line of Python" lesson as the structural parallel to Jacquard's loom that Ada Lovelace first observed [sources/foundation-2-2-one-line-of-python]

## Relationships

- Collaborated conceptually with [[ada-lovelace]], who annotated the Analytical Engine and wrote the first algorithm
- Used [[joseph-marie-jacquard]]'s punched-card scheme as the control primitive
