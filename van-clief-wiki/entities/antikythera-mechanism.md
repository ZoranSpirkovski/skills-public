# antikythera-mechanism

Ancient Greek astronomical computer, dated c. 100-150 BCE, recovered from a shipwreck in 1900. Used epicyclic gearing (gears on gears) to compute astronomical positions. Decoded in 2021 as a device that "mechanized the predictions of scientific theories". Van Clief cites it as proof that sophisticated mechanical computation predates Heron by ~200 years.

## Claims

- Calculated ecliptic longitudes for Moon, Sun, and the five visible planets [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Predicted eclipses using the 18.2-year Saros cycle [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Accounted for the Moon's elliptical orbit ~17 centuries before Kepler formalized it [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Used differential gearing, a technology normally credited to the Industrial Revolution [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- 2021 analysis concluded it could have "automated many of the calculations needed for its own design" [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
