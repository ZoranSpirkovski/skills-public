# alphazero

Google DeepMind's self-taught game-playing AI. Starting from only the rules, mastered chess in 4 hours, shogi in 2 hours, and surpassed the original [[alphago]] at Go in 30 hours - with no human games used for training. Searched 80,000 positions per second against [[stockfish]]'s 70 million and played stronger - "not calculating more; it was understanding better."

## Claims

- Tabula rasa: no human games, only rules and self-play [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Mastered chess in 4 hours, shogi in 2, exceeded AlphaGo at Go in 30 [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Searched ~900x fewer positions than Stockfish yet played stronger [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- [[garry-kasparov]]: "It's like discovering the secret notebooks of some great player from the past" [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Peter Heine Nielsen (Magnus Carlsen's coach): "I always wondered how it would be if a superior species landed on earth and showed us how they play chess. I feel now I know." [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
