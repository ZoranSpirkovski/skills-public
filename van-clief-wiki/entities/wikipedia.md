# wikipedia

Online encyclopedia. Used as the comparison baseline in the apocalypse-laptop thought experiment from Lesson 3.3. Wikipedia retrieves what already exists; its infrastructure is extremely efficient, at about 0.0000118 kWh per page view. Van Clief uses it as the strong case for the retrieval-vs-synthesis distinction: even a 25x energy disadvantage for AI is an apples-to-oranges comparison because AI synthesizes new, situation-specific answers rather than serving static articles.

## Claims

- 2021 global server infrastructure consumed 3.143 GWh serving 265 billion page views [sources/archive-3-3-real-cost-of-knowledge]
- About 0.0000118 kWh per page view [sources/archive-3-3-real-cost-of-knowledge]
- Can only return what already exists; every new article, edit, or update requires human energy [sources/archive-3-3-real-cost-of-knowledge]
- Used as the losing side of Van Clief's apocalypse-laptop thought experiment once the comparison metric shifts from energy-per-query to energy-per-insight [sources/archive-3-3-real-cost-of-knowledge]
