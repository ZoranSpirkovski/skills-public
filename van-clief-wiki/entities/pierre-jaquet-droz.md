# pierre-jaquet-droz

Swiss 18th-century clockmaker whose family produced the most sophisticated automata of the era. The Writer (1772) contains over 6,000 parts, is still programmable today (selector wheel for any text up to 40 characters), dips its quill, shakes its wrist to prevent dripping, and follows the text with its eyes as it writes.

## Claims

- Completed the Writer in 1772; 6,000+ parts; still operational, on display at the Musée d'Art et d'Histoire in Geneva [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- On the first Sunday of each month, the Writer still produces messages; it sometimes writes "I think therefore I am" - a direct staging of [[rene-descartes]]'s test [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- Van Clief presents the Writer as the pivot where the question shifts from "can machines think?" to "what's the difference between thinking and perfectly imitating thought?" [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
