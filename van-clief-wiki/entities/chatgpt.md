# chatgpt

OpenAI's consumer-facing chat product on top of the GPT family of models. Appears in Archive Lessons as the dominant public reference point for "AI" in late 2023 and 2024, and as the energy benchmark in Lesson 3.3. A ChatGPT query draws roughly 0.0003 kWh, about 25 times more than a Wikipedia page view. GPT-3 training consumed 1,287 MWh and 700,000 liters of fresh water.

## Claims

- A ChatGPT query uses about 0.0003 kWh [sources/archive-3-3-real-cost-of-knowledge]
- A GPT-4 query uses about 0.0005 kWh [sources/archive-3-3-real-cost-of-knowledge]
- GPT-3 training used 1,287 MWh, enough to power 120 American homes for a year, and 700,000 liters of fresh water [sources/archive-3-3-real-cost-of-knowledge]
