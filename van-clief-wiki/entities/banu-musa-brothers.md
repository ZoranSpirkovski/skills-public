# banu-musa-brothers

9th-century Baghdad engineers and authors of the *Book of Ingenious Devices* (c. 850 CE). Van Clief cites them for what he considers the conceptual leap from "machine that does one thing" to "machine that does what you tell it" - user-programmable automation, decades before Heron's heirs in Europe.

## Claims

- Described a steam-powered flute player whose melodies could be reconfigured by the user [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]
- The conceptual innovation was user-controlled programmability, not just automation [sources/archive-1-2-2000-year-overnight-success]

## Relationships

- Precede [[al-jazari]] in the Baghdad-to-Middle-East automaton lineage
- Cited in sequence with [[heron-of-alexandria]] and [[antikythera-mechanism]] as evidence of pre-industrial programmable machines
