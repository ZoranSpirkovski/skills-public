# intel-pentium

Intel's Pentium processor line, cited in Archive 1.2 as part of the thread on computing reliability being engineered *over* an unreliable substrate. Thomas Nicely discovered the FDIV bug in 1994 (five missing entries in the chip's division lookup table), forcing a $475M recall. Used to illustrate that even deterministic-seeming hardware has failure modes that have to be handled architecturally.
